# Escalation Guidance

> Source: [Configuration Document for SPOT NPEWS (NHS England)](reference-sources/Configuration%20Document%20for%20SPOT%20NPEWS.pdf)

---

## Escalation Level: Low

| Trigger | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| **Specific Concern** | None |
| **Clinical Intuition** | Nurse/Clinician concern that patient needs increased monitoring despite low PEWS |
| **Carer Question** | Carer uses words that suggests the child needs increased monitoring or intervention despite the low PEWS |
| **PEWS Score** | 1 – 4 |

**Communication and Response:**

- Inform Nurse in Charge
- Consider medical review by ST3+ or equivalent
- Bedside Nurse to feedback plan to parents

**Medical Review Timings:** As agreed with medical team

**Minimal Observations:** Must reassess within 60 minutes (and then document ongoing plan)

---

## Escalation Level: Medium

| Trigger | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| **Specific Concern** | New suspicion of sepsis |
| **Clinical Intuition** | Nurse/Clinician concern that patient needs a medical review irrespective of PEWS |
| **Carer Question** | Carer uses words that suggests the child needs a clinical review irrespective of PEWS |
| **PEWS Score** | 5 – 8 |

**Communication and Response:**

- Review by Nurse in charge for potential escalation (and/or Outreach Nurse or equivalent)
- Request Medical Review by ST3+ or equivalent
- Stabilisation plan to be considered
- Bedside Nurse to feedback to parents

**Medical Review Timings:** Within 30 minutes

**Minimal Observations:** Must reassess within 30 minutes (and then document ongoing plan), Continuous Oxygen Saturation Monitoring needed

---

## Escalation Level: High

| Trigger | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| **Specific Concern** | AVPU: Change to AVPU – V "Responsive only to Voice" or New suspicion of septic shock |
| **Clinical Intuition** | Nurse/Clinician concern that patient needs a 'Rapid Review' irrespective of PEWS |
| **Carer Question** | Carer uses words that suggests the child needs a 'Rapid Review' irrespective of PEWS |
| **PEWS Score** | 9 – 12 |

**Communication and Response:**

- Immediate review by Nurse in Charge for potential escalation
- Call for 'Rapid Review': Medical incl. airway skills ST3+ and outreach nurse (If available or equivalent)
- Stabilisation plan to be discussed with consultant
- Senior nurse to feedback plan to parents

**Medical Review Timings:** Within 15 minutes

**Minimal Observations:** Every 30 minutes and continuous monitoring of Respiratory Rate / Oxygen Saturation / ECG, GCS recording if change in AVPU

---

## Escalation Level: Emergency

| Trigger | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| **Specific Concern** | Change to AVPU – P or U "Responsive only to Pain" or "Unresponsive" or Abnormal pupillary response |
| **Clinical Intuition** | Nurse/Clinician concern that patient needs emergency review for life-threatening situation |
| **Carer Question** | Carer uses words that suggests the child has collapsed or significantly deteriorated |
| **PEWS Score** | ≥13 |

**Communication and Response:**

- Immediate 2222 call: "Paediatric Medical Emergency" and review by Nurse in Charge
- Consultant informed urgently to confirm stabilisation plan
- Senior nurse to support and feedback to parents
- [In specialist environments rapid review can replace 2222 but only with prior agreement between consultant and Nurse in Charge]

**Medical Review Timings:** Immediate

**Minimal Observations:** Every 15 minutes and continuous monitoring of Respiratory Rate / Oxygen Saturation / ECG, GCS recording if change in AVPU or abnormal pupillary response

## Escalation Options for Clinical Intuition

- **Yes** – Concerned
- **No** – Not Concerned

If "Yes" is selected, the clinician then has the option to escalate to any level:

- None
- Low
- Medium
- High
- Emergency

## Escalation Options for Parent / Carer Question

- **W** – Worse
- **S** – Same
- **B** – Better
- **A** – Parent/Carer Asleep
- **U** – Unavailable

If "Worse" is selected, the clinician then has the option to escalate to any level:

- None
- Low
- Medium
- High
- Emergency
